Vaccination for children: Protect your child from diseases

  • Protect your child from diseases
  • Getting comfortable with vaccinating
  • When to vaccinate
  • Supporting your child
  • For Indigenous **content needed

Vaccines help protect your child from illness and they can help stop illness from spreading to those around you.

Here is a list of vaccine-preventable diseases, their symptoms and potential complications.

Diseases that can be prevented with vaccines

  • Chickenpox (Varicella)

    Symptoms of disease

    • fever
    • many small blisters that develop scabs
    • itching

    Possible complications

    • ear infection
    • pneumonia
    • skin infection (e.g. impetigo), sometimes severe (e.g. flesh-eating disease)
    • encephalitis (brain infection)
    • malformations in infants in cases where mother was infected during pregnancy
    • shingles (15-30% over lifetime)
    • death
  • Diphtheria<

    Symptoms of disease

    • high feve
    • severe sore throat
    • respiratory and heart problems

    Possible complications

    • paralysis
    • death in 5-10% of cases
